## Prerequisites

Before starting the integration, ensure you have:

- Active Plivo account with API access
- Auth ID and Auth Token from Plivo Console
- At least one Plivo phone number
- Active Ringg workspace with admin access

## Step 1: Plivo Account Setup

### Create a Plivo Account

1. Sign up at [Plivo Console](https://console.plivo.com)
2. Complete account verification process
3. Add credits to your account for phone number rental and calls

### Obtain API Credentials

Navigate to your Plivo dashboard and locate your API credentials:

<CodeGroup>
```bash Credentials
AUTH_ID=MAZJ*************LHYT
AUTH_TOKEN=YTQwZ*******************I5MzM2
```
</CodeGroup>

![Plivo Dashboard](/images/plivo_dashboard.png)

## Step 2: Phone Number Configuration

### Purchase a Phone Number

1. Navigate to **Phone Numbers** → **Buy Numbers** in Plivo Console
2. Select your preferred country and number type:
   - **Local Numbers**: Best for regional presence
   - **Toll-free Numbers**: Professional appearance for customer service
   - **Mobile Numbers**: Available in select countries
3. Complete the purchase

### Configure Voice Settings

Your Plivo numbers will be automatically configured for voice calls when you add them to Ringg.

## Step 3: Connect Plivo to Ringg

### Add Plivo as Your Telephony Provider

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Integrations">
    Go to your Ringg dashboard and click **Integrations** → **Telephony Integration**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Plivo Provider">
    Click **"Add Telephony Provider"** and select **Plivo**

 ![BYOT Provider Selection](/images/byot_provider_selection.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Credentials">
    Fill in your Plivo credentials:
    ![Provider Credentials Form](/images/provider_creds.png)
    - **Auth ID**: Your Plivo Auth ID
    - **Auth Token**: Your Plivo Auth Token

  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Connection & Save">
    Click **"Test Connection"** to verify your credentials are working and save them
  </Step>
</Steps>


Now provider is added to your ringg workspace 

![Provider Successfully Added](/images/provider_added.png)

## Step 5: Test Your Integration

### Test Inbound Calls

1. **Basic Functionality Test**
   - Call your Plivo number from any phone
   - Verify the AI assistant answers and responds appropriately
   - Check that the conversation flows naturally

2. **Dashboard Verification**
   - Monitor the call in your Ringg dashboard
   - Verify call logs appear in both Ringg and Plivo
   - Check that recordings and transcriptions are generated

### Test Call Quality

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Audio Quality Checklist">

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Connection Issues">
    **Problem**: Unable to connect Plivo account to Ringg
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Verify Auth ID and Auth Token are correct
    - Ensure Plivo account has sufficient credits
    - Check that API access is enabled in Plivo console
    - Verify account is not suspended or restricted
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Import Problems">
    **Problem**: Numbers not appearing during import
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Confirm numbers are active in your Plivo account
    - Check that numbers have voice capabilities enabled
    - Verify regional availability (some numbers may be restricted)
    - Ensure proper account permissions for API access
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Call Quality Issues">
    **Problem**: Poor audio quality or dropped calls
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Check your internet connection stability
    - Verify Plivo service status for your region
    - Test with different phone numbers and devices
    - Contact Plivo support if issues persist regionally
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="AI Assistant Not Responding">
    **Problem**: Calls connect but AI assistant doesn't respond
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Verify assistant is properly assigned to the number
    - Check assistant configuration and status
    - Review webhook logs for any errors
    - Ensure assistant has proper knowledge base setup
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
